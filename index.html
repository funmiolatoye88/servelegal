Where this goes next
The roadmap: from risk briefing
to compliance intelligence layer
The current platform works with ServeLegal audit outcomes and staffing signals. But there is a richer data source that most retailers already have and are not using for compliance intelligence: point-of-sale override data. Every time a POS system flags an age-restricted item and a staff member dismisses the prompt without checking ID, that event is logged. That data, connected to this platform, transforms the risk model entirely — from predicting who might fail to identifying stores with a systemic culture of override. Here is what the product roadmap looks like.
Now
Audit outcome intelligence
Predict which stores will fail their next audit based on staffing, tenure, calendar, and historical patterns. Deliver ranked interventions before the audit window opens.
Live POC
Next — 6 to 12 months
POS override integration
Connect to client POS systems to ingest prompt dismissal data. Stores with high override rates are flagged as culture risk, not just staffing risk. A fundamentally stronger predictive signal.
In roadmap
Future — 12 to 24 months
Compliance behaviour exchange
Anonymised, aggregated override and failure data across the full client estate becomes an industry benchmarking product. Clients pay to know how their compliance culture compares to sector peers.

Under the hood
How the AI thinks,
step by step
There's no black box here. This page walks you through exactly what the AI is doing at every stage: from the moment it ingests your data, to the moment it hands you a ranked list of things to do today. Plain English, no technical jargon.
Step 1 · Data collection
The AI reads everything it knows about your stores
Before it can spot risk, it needs to understand the full picture. Not just last week's audit, but everything that shapes whether a store is likely to pass or fail right now.
What the AI is actually doing
It pulls together every data signal it has access to: audit history going back years, current staffing rosters, staff tenure records, training completion dates, footfall patterns, school proximity, upcoming calendar events, and manager cover schedules. Think of it as building a complete health check for every store simultaneously.
Audit history
Every pass/fail result, product category, time of day, auditor notes
Staff data
Tenure days, training dates, shift patterns, manager availability
Calendar signals
Bank holidays, school terms, local events, extended trading hours
In plain English
Imagine hiring a compliance analyst who never sleeps, reads every document ever written about your stores, and updates their knowledge every single day. That's what's happening here: except it takes seconds, not weeks.
Step 2 · Pattern recognition
It finds the hidden patterns in 20 years of failure data
This is where the intelligence lives. The AI has studied 250,000+ audits and found the conditions that reliably predict a failed mystery shop: long before the auditor walks through the door.
What the AI is actually doing
It runs statistical analysis across decades of audit outcomes, cross-referencing every variable it can measure. It asks: when stores failed, what did they have in common? It found that certain factors reliably cluster around failures: and it quantified exactly how much risk each factor adds.
New staff
Under 90 days tenure → +45% failure rate vs experienced staff
No manager
No manager on the floor → +25% failure rate, every time
School proximity
Near a school, Friday afternoon → +20% additional risk loading
Overdue training
Training lapsed 180+ days → +30% failure rate
Late shifts
Friday/Saturday evenings → +20% elevated risk window
High footfall
Peak trading periods → +15% stress on compliance behaviour
In plain English
It's like a doctor who's studied thousands of patient records and can now look at your test results and say: "These three things together: that's how it usually starts." Except instead of health, it's compliance. And instead of symptoms, it's staffing patterns.
Step 3 · Risk scoring
Every store gets a live risk score, updated continuously
The AI doesn't just flag stores as risky. It scores every single store on a 0–100 scale, weights the factors by their proven impact, and recalculates whenever something changes.
What the AI is actually doing
For each store, it loads the current conditions: who's working, how long they've been there, when training was last completed, what's happening in the calendar: and multiplies each factor by its known risk weight. These scores stack up to produce a single number that tells you exactly where each store sits on the risk spectrum right now.

Step 4 · Forward projection
It looks ahead and flags what's coming: not just what's happening now
A store might look fine today but become high risk on Friday. The AI projects risk forward across the next 14 days, factoring in everything it knows is coming: staff changes, holidays, calendar events, audit windows.
What the AI is actually doing
It simulates how each store's risk profile will evolve over the coming days. If a new cohort of staff joins Wednesday, it knows those individuals' tenure clock starts then. If Easter weekend is approaching, it loads the historical failure rate uplift for that period. It then surfaces the windows where risk peaks: giving you time to act before the window opens.
Calendar modelling
Bank holidays, school breaks, peak trading periods all loaded automatically
Tenure progression
Knows which new starters cross the 90-day threshold and when
Audit window awareness
Knows when auditors are scheduled and overlays risk timing
In plain English
It's the difference between a weather app that tells you it's raining now, and one that tells you Friday is going to be a storm: pack an umbrella on Thursday. You get time to prepare. That's the whole point.
Step 5 · Plain-English translation
It turns the data into a briefing any manager can understand and act on
Data is useless if people can't act on it. The AI translates every risk signal and score into clear, jargon-free language: automatically generating the weekly manager briefing, ready to send.
What the AI is actually doing
It takes the risk scores, the pattern analysis, and the forward projection: and writes a weekly briefing document in plain English. It identifies the top 3 things a store manager needs to know, explains why they matter in terms any frontline person understands, and formats it for immediate distribution. No analyst required. No report-writing hours wasted. Done automatically, every week.
In plain English
Think of it as a brilliant ops director who reads all the data, understands what it means, and writes the morning briefing for your store managers every single Monday morning: without being asked, and without getting it wrong.
Output: A ready-to-send manager briefing, auto-generated from live data. Personalised per store. Available every week without manual effort.
Step 6 · Intervention ranking
It tells you exactly what to do: ranked by what will save the most points
This is where it stops being an insight tool and becomes an action tool. The AI doesn't just show you the risk. It works backwards from the risk score and identifies the specific actions that will reduce it the most: ranked, quantified, and ready to execute.
What the AI is actually doing
For each store and each risk factor, it runs the calculation in reverse: if this factor contributes 24 points to the risk score, then removing this factor saves 24 points. It then ranks every possible intervention by its point-reduction impact, filters for actions that are realistic to execute before the risk window opens, and presents them as a clear, ordered to-do list. Highest impact first. Always.
Confirm manager cover
Removes the largest single risk factor. −24 points immediately.
Buddy-pair new starters
Mitigates tenure risk without changing rosters. −18 points.
Run a team briefing
10 minutes of awareness. −15 points per location.
Output: A ranked action list with predicted point savings per intervention. Total potential reduction: 30+ risk points before the audit window opens.
